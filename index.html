About Us

Devanand B Meshram an ex-servicemen is riding the length of India from sky i.e. lal chowk Srinagar to heaven cape comorin, kanyakumari on his bicycle starting 09 oct 2022. The main purpose of mission Kashmir to kanyakumari is to spread the message to motivate youngsters to join the armed forces, getting Indians of all ages active for a healthy future by using cycle to work, cycle to office and cycle to market.

Devanand was not financially so strong for this ride. He is class 3 employee but with the support of class mates and peoples of warud, dist-amravati Maharashtra state and other places, they sponsored him and thus able to start this ride. Also for this mission kendriya sainik board Delhi, Department of sainik welfare pune has taken the initiative to arrange the accommodation and other help during his entire ride from Kashmir to kanyakumari.

Devanand will be riding daily 300 km approx. for about 18-19 hrs. on an average. Only 4 to 5 hours for other things, sleep, freshening up and other essential chores. Devanand belongs to small town warud of Amravati district in Maharashtra state also called as vidarbhs California which is famous for oranges. After serving the nation for 15 yrs in Indian Navy, now he is working as talathi in revenue department under Maharashtra state government.

Before this event he has done many long distance races. The toughest was climb ka baap 1200 km brevet at elevation of 34000 feet from delhi to dharamshala organized by delhi randonneur club. He is then only rider from Maharashtra to complete successfully this brevet. Also he completed the 5 days SR series at jintur dist. parbhani i.e 1500 kms in 5 days. Again only rider from Maharashtra to do so. Till now he became 5 times super randonneur. Apart from this he is active in social activities like, blood donations, plantation and water conservation. In 2016 he bag the styamev jayte water cup prize of Rs. 25 lakhs for his village wathoda in Maharashtra organized by cine star amir khan paani foundation. For his contribution he was felicitated in Mumbai on the hands of amir khan and then CM DEVENDRAJI FADNAVIS.

Devanand also completed the India's toughest ultra cycling race deccan cliff hanger from pune to goa. He started cycling 5 yrs back with the intention of cycling 3 days in a week for going to office /other places. But now it has become passion for him. He is a motivator for others and promoting cycling in and around warud/Amravati/Nagpur city.
